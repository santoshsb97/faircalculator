Know Your Worth: Salary Guide

The True Value of Your Time

When you look at a job offer, the annual salary is the headline number—but it doesn't tell the whole story. Breaking it down into hourly, weekly, or even daily rates changes your perspective. Is that $60,000/year job worth it if it demands 60 hours a week? Our calculator helps you find the real hourly wage you're earning.

Hourly vs. Salary: The Pros and Cons

  • Hourly: You get paid for every minute you work. Overtime (usually 1.5x pay) is a huge perk. If you work late, you get rewarded.
  • Salary: Stability is the main benefit. You get the same paycheck whether business is slow or busy. However, you might work late nights without extra pay.

Negotiation Tip: The "Bi-Weekly" Effect

Most corporate jobs pay bi-weekly (every 2 weeks). This means there are two months in the year where you get three paychecks instead of two. Savvy budgeters use these "bonus" checks for savings or vacations, living strictly off the standard two checks per month.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does a 40-hour week translate to annual hours?

The standard formula is 40 hours × 52 weeks = 2,080 hours per year. Use this number to quickly convert any hourly rate to an annual salary (e.g., $25/hr × 2080 = $52,000).

What is the difference between Gross and Net pay?

Gross Pay is the big number on your offer letter. Net Pay is what actually hits your bank account after taxes, insurance, and retirement contributions are taken out.

Why calculate a daily rate?

Knowing your daily rate helps with freelance work or negotiating per-diem rates. It's also a great motivator—knowing you earn $200 a day can make that morning coffee run feel more affordable!
