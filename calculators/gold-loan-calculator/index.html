Understanding Gold Valuation

Calculating the value of a gold loan isn't as simple as checking the daily ticker. Lenders look at the "melt value" of your items—the intrinsic value of the gold if it were to be melted down and purified. Our Gold Loan Estimator helps you approximate this by accounting for weight, purity, and the current market spread.

Key Valuation Factors

  • Purity (Karat): 24K is 99.9% pure, while 18K is 75% pure gold mixed with alloys for strength.
  • Troy Ounces vs Grams: The global market trades in Troy Ounces (approx 31.1g), while jewelry is measured in grams.
  • Market Spread: Lenders usually lend against the 'Bid' price, which is slightly lower than the 'Ask' price you see on news sites.
  • Stones & Filigree: Lenders subtract the weight of gemstones and non-gold elements before valuing the metal.

Why Borrow Against Gold?

  • Speed: Often approved in minutes compared to days for personal loans.
  • No Credit Friction: Your asset is the security; your credit score is rarely the deciding factor.
  • Lower Interest: Generally cheaper than credit cards because it is a secured loan.
  • Retain Ownership: You get your property back once the debt is settled.

A Historical Perspective

Gold has served as the ultimate store of value for millennia. From the Gold Standard that once backed global currencies to modern pawn lending, the stability of gold makes it the preferred collateral during times of market volatility. By leveraging your gold, you're tapping into a source of liquidity that doesn't require selling your long-term hedge against inflation.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Loan-to-Value (LTV) ratio?

LTV represents the percentage of your gold's value that the lender is willing to lend. If your gold is worth $1,000 and the LTV is 70%, you can borrow $700. Regulators often set caps on LTV to protect both lenders and borrowers from price drops.

Do I lose my gold if I miss a payment?

Missing a payment usually triggers late fees. However, if you default entirely, the lender has the legal right to auction your gold to recover the principal and interest. Always borrow only what you can comfortably repay.

Does it affect my credit score?

Standard gold loans from local shops usually don't involve credit reporting. However, loans from formal banks may report your repayment history to credit bureaus, helping you build credit if paid on time.
