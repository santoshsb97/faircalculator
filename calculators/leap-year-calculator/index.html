Leap Year Calculator: Check If a Year Is a Leap Year

A Leap Year Calculator helps you quickly determine whether a specific year is a leap year or not. Leap years play an important role in keeping our calendar aligned with the Earth’s movement around the Sun. By adding an extra day—February 29—every few years, the calendar stays accurate over long periods of time.

Using the leap year calculator above, you can instantly check a single year or list leap years within a range, without having to remember complex rules or perform manual calculations.

What Is a Leap Year?

A leap year is a calendar year that contains 366 days instead of the usual 365 days. The extra day is added to the month of February, which becomes 29 days long instead of 28.

This adjustment is necessary because the Earth does not orbit the Sun in exactly 365 days. One full orbit takes approximately 365.2422 days. Without leap years, our calendar would slowly drift out of sync with the seasons.

Why Do Leap Years Exist?

Leap years exist to correct the small difference between the calendar year and the astronomical year. If we ignored this difference:

  • Seasons would slowly shift over time
  • Calendars would become inaccurate
  • Important dates tied to seasons would drift

By adding one extra day every few years, leap years ensure long-term accuracy.

Leap Year Rules (Simple Explanation)

A year is a leap year if it follows these rules:

  • The year must be divisible by 4
  • If the year is divisible by 100, it is NOT a leap year
  • Unless the year is also divisible by 400

Examples:

  • 2024 → Leap year ✅ (divisible by 4)
  • 1900 → Not a leap year ❌ (divisible by 100, not by 400)
  • 2000 → Leap year ✅ (divisible by 400)

These rules ensure precise long-term calendar alignment.

Why February 29 Matters

February 29 only exists in leap years. People born on this date are often called “leap day babies” or “leaplings.”

In non-leap years, some celebrate on February 28, while others celebrate on March 1. The leap year calculator helps determine when February 29 will occur next.

How Often Do Leap Years Occur?

Most leap years occur every 4 years, but the century rule slightly adjusts this pattern. Over a 400-year cycle, there are 97 leap years. This keeps the calendar extremely accurate.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What years are leap years?

Leap years are years divisible by 4, except for century years unless they are divisible by 400.

Is 2025 a leap year?

No. 2025 is not divisible by 4, so it is not a leap year.

Is 2100 a leap year?

No. Although divisible by 100, it is not divisible by 400.

How many days are in a leap year?

A leap year has 366 days.

Does every country follow the same leap year rules?

Yes. Leap year rules are standardized under the Gregorian calendar.
