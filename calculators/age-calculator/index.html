❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. How old am I today?

Simply enter your date of birth into our age finder, and we will calculate my current age instantly in years, months, and days.

2. What is my age in days?

Our age calculator in days functionality shows you the total number of days you've lived since your birth date.

3. Can I find someone's age by their birthday?

Yes! This tool works as a date of birth calculator to help you find out how old someone is without manual math.

4. How do you calculate chronological age?

To calculate chronological age, you simply subtract the birth date from the current date. Our chronological age calculator does this with day-level precision, accounting for leap years and different month lengths.

5. What is the difference between biological and chronological age?

While chronological age is the amount of time since you were born, biological age refers to how old your cells and tissues are. You can use specialized health tools to see if your body is aging faster or slower than your calendar age.

6. How is gestational age calculated?

A gestational age calculator by lmp (last menstrual period) starts the clock from the first day of your last cycle. Alternatively, a gestational age calculator by edd (estimated due date) works backward from your predicted delivery date to estimate gestational age.

7. What is corrected age for premature babies?

Corrected age (or adjusted age for prematurity calculator) is used for babies born before 37 weeks. It is calculated by subtracting the number of weeks they were born early from their chronological age. You can use our preemie adjusted age calculator to ensure your child is hitting their developmental milestones correctly.

8. When is my full retirement age?

Your full retirement age depends on your birth year calculator results. For most people born after 1960, it is 67. You can use a retirement age calculator by date of birth or social security benefits calculator by age to find the exact date you become eligible for full benefits.

9. How does a life expectancy calculator work?

A life expectancy calculator uses actuarial data and health metrics to estimate life expectancy. These tools consider your current age, gender, and lifestyle to predict how many more years you might live.
