Understanding Highest Common Factor (HCF)

The Highest Common Factor (HCF), also known as the Greatest Common Divisor (GCD), of two or more integers is the largest positive integer that divides each of the numbers without leaving a remainder. Our HCF calculator is a powerful HCF finder that helps you calculate HCF of any number set instantly.

Example: HCF(12, 18) = 6 because 6 is the largest number that divides both 12 and 18. This GCD calculator is optimized to find the HCF for both small and large inputs.

Comparison of HCF Methods

  • Listing Factors: Best for beginners and small numbers. A simple common factor finder approach.
  • Prime Factorization: Finding HCF using prime factorization is highly reliable for any set of numbers. It breaks numbers into building blocks.
  • Euclidean Algorithm: The most efficient mathematical way to calculate HCF for two large numbers.
  • Cake Method: A popular visual hcf calculator with steps for finding factors of multiple numbers simultaneously.

Find HCF & GCF: Step-by-Step Examples

To help you understand how to calculate HCF using different methods, here are some common problems solved step-by-step:

Listing Method

GCF of 12 and 18:

  • Factors of 12: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12
  • Factors of 18: 1, 2, 3, 6, 9, 18
  • The highest common factor is 6.

Prime Factorization

GCF of 16 and 24:

  • 16 = 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 (2⁴)
  • 24 = 2 × 2 × 2 × 3 (2³ × 3)
  • Common: 2³ = 8.

Euclidean Algorithm

GCF of 40 and 48:

  • 48 = 40 × 1 + 8
  • 40 = 8 × 5 + 0
  • The last non-zero remainder is 8.

Cake/Ladder Method

GCF of 8 and 12:

Divide both by 2 to get 4 and 6. Divide again by 2 to get 2 and 3. Multiply the divisors: 2 × 2 = 4.

Common Query

GCF of 18 and 24:

Both 18 and 24 are divisible by 6 (their largest common divisor). Therefore, HCF(18, 24) = 6.

HCF & LCM Relationship

The formula of hcf and lcm for two numbers a and b is: a × b = HCF(a,b) × LCM(a,b).

This lcm hcf formula is useful for hcf and lcm calculator logic. If you know the LCM, you can find the HCF using: HCF = (Product of Numbers) / LCM.

Most Asked HCF & GCF Questions

Below are some of the most common queries regarding how to find the greatest common factor for various number sets and algebraic expressions.

How to find the common factor of 4, 8, and 12?
To find the common factor of 4 8 and 12, list their factors: 4 (1, 2, 4), 8 (1, 2, 4, 8), 12 (1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12). The common factors are 1, 2, and 4. The GCF is 4.
Find the common factor of 56 and 120?
For 56 and 120: Prime factors of 56 are 2³ × 7. Prime factors of 120 are 2³ × 3 × 5. The common part is 2³, so the GCF is 8.
How to find the factors and identify the GCF?
To find the factors and identify the gcf, first list all numbers that divide each value perfectly. For example, to find the factors and identify the gcf 15 and 45: Factors of 15 (1, 3, 5, 15), Factors of 45 (1, 3, 5, 9, 15, 45). The highest common factor is 15.
What is "find the gcf math"?
In mathematics, find the gcf math refers to finding the largest number that divides a set of integers without a remainder. It is the fundamental process for simplifying fractions and solving ratio problems.
How to find the GCF of 12 and 15?
To find the gcf of 12 and 15 or find the greatest common factor of 12 and 15: 12 (1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12), 15 (1, 3, 5, 15). The common factor is 3. GCF = 3.
What is the GCF of 18 and 35?
To find the greatest common factor of 18 and 35: 18 = 2 × 3², 35 = 5 × 7. They share no prime factors other than 1. Thus, the GCF is 1. These numbers are called "coprime."
How to find the GCF of 22 and 48?
To find the greatest common factor of 22 and 48: 22 = 2 × 11, 48 = 2⁴ × 3. The only common factor is 2.
How to find the GCF of 28 and 48?
To find the greatest common factor of 28 and 48: 28 = 2² × 7, 48 = 2⁴ × 3. The common factor is 2², so the GCF is 4.
How to find the GCF of 7, 15, and 21?
To find the greatest common factor of 7 15 and 21: 7 is prime. 15 = 3 × 5. 21 = 3 × 7. Since no common factor exists for all three other than 1, the GCF is 1.
How to find the GCF with variables and exponents?
To find the gcf with variables and exponents, take the lowest power of each common variable. For example:
  • Find the gcf of 10y and 12xy: HCF of 10, 12 is 2. Common variable is y. GCF = 2y.
  • Find the gcf of x4 x7: The lowest power of x is 4. GCF = x⁴.
  • Find the gcf of 4y and 12y: GCF is 4y.
  • Find the gcf of 15y6 and 9z: Coefficients (15, 9) have HCF 3. No common variables. GCF = 3.
How to find the GCF of polynomials?
To find the greatest common factor of polynomials or find the gcf of the terms of each polynomial, you identify the greatest factor that divides every term. For example, in 28d³ + 32d³ + 20d⁴, the coefficients (28, 32, 20) have an HCF of 4, and the lowest power of d is d³. So, find the gcf of 28d3 32d3 and 20d4 result is 4d³.
