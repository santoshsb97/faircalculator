How to Calculate Salary Sacrifice Tax Savings

Calculating your salary sacrifice 2026 savings involves comparing your tax and NI liability at two different gross salary points. The formula is essentially:

Total Saving = (Income Tax Base - Income Tax Adjusted) + (Employee NI Base - Employee NI Adjusted)
  1. Find your Taxable Income: Subtract your Personal Allowance (£12,570) from your Gross Pay.
  2. Apply Tax Bands: Calculate tax on your base salary, then repeat for your salary after the sacrifice is removed.
  3. Calculate Employee NI: Apply the relevant NI rates (e.g., 8%) to earnings above the Primary Threshold.
  4. The Net Difference: Your take-home pay is reduced, but the tax/NI savings act as a "subsidy" for your pension boost.

Key Definitions

Personal Allowance Taper

For every £2 your "Adjusted Net Income" exceeds £100,000, you lose £1 of your Personal Allowance. This creates an effective tax rate of 60% in the £100k-£125k bracket.

NI Category Letters

Most employees are Category A. Category C is for those over State Pension age, while M and H provide Employer NI relief for young workers.

Secondary Threshold

The point at which employers begin paying National Insurance on your earnings. For 2026-27, this is significantly lower (~£5,000) than the employee threshold.

Effective Cost per £1

If a £100 pension contribution only reduces your take-home pay by £70, your "effective cost" is 70p per £1. Salary sacrifice is the only way to achieve these efficiencies instantly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is salary sacrifice legal for everyone?

Yes, it is a legitimate HMRC-approved path for pension contributions. However, your hourly pay must not fall below the National Minimum Wage after the sacrifice is applied.

Will this affect my mortgage application?

It can. Mortgage lenders often look at your "contractual gross salary." Since salary sacrifice legally reduces this, it might slightly lower your borrowing limit, though many lenders are now comfortable with "pension add-backs."

What happens to my NI savings in 2029?

From April 2029, the government plans to cap the National Insurance savings available through salary sacrifice. This means the high efficiency currently available will be reduced for most workers.

Do I get tax relief on top of salary sacrifice?

No. Salary sacrifice achieves tax relief by reducing your pay before tax is taken. This is generally more efficient than "relief at source" because you save National Insurance as well.
