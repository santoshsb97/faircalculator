Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana Calculator: Plan Your Daughter's Future in 2026

Invest ₹50,000 yearly at 8.2% interest and get ₹23.94 lakh after 21 years

The Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana (SSY) calculator is your go-to tool for planning your daughter's financial future. With the current interest rate of 8.2% per annum (as of 2026), this government-backed scheme remains one of the best investment options for Indian parents.

Let's break down exactly how this calculator works and why it matters for your family.

What is Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana?

Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana is a small savings scheme launched by the Government of India under the Beti Bachao Beti Padhao campaign. It's designed specifically to build a secure financial future for girl children.

Key Highlights at a Glance

Feature Details
Current Interest Rate 8.2% p.a. (2026)
Minimum Investment ₹250 per year
Maximum Investment ₹1.5 lakh per year
Tenure 21 years
Tax Benefit Up to ₹1.5 lakh under Section 80C
Eligibility Age Girl child below 10 years

How the SSY Calculator Works: Real Example

Based on the calculation shown above, here's what happens when you invest ₹50,000 annually:

Investment Breakdown

  • Total Amount Invested: ₹7.50 lakh (over 15 years)
  • Interest Earned: ₹16.44 lakh
  • Maturity Value: ₹23.94 lakh (in year 2047)
  • Return Ratio: 68.7% comes from interest alone
Why This Matters: Out of every ₹100 you get at maturity, ₹69 is pure interest income. That's the power of compounding working for you over 21 years.

The calculator auto-fills the current government rate (8.2% for 2026). This rate is reviewed quarterly.

Eligibility Criteria: Who Can Open SSY Account?

✅ You're Eligible If:

  1. Girl child is below 10 years at account opening
  2. Indian resident (NRI accounts not allowed)
  3. One account per girl child (maximum 2 daughters)
  4. Exception: Third account allowed if second birth results in twins

❌ Not Eligible If:

  • Girl child is 10+ years old
  • Already opened 2 accounts for other daughters
  • Non-resident Indian (NRI) status

Tax Benefits: Triple Advantage

SSY offers EEE (Exempt-Exempt-Exempt) tax status:

1. Investment Deduction

Claim up to ₹1.5 lakh under Section 80C annually

2. Interest Tax-Free

The 8.2% interest earned is completely tax-free

3. Maturity Tax-Free

Final withdrawal after 21 years is 100% tax-free

Example: If you invest ₹50,000 yearly, you save up to ₹15,600 in taxes annually (assuming 30% tax bracket).

Important Rules You Must Know

Deposit Rules

  • Minimum: ₹250 per year (reduced from ₹1,000)
  • Maximum: ₹1.5 lakh per year
  • Deposits: Can make lump sum or multiple installments
  • Deadline: Must deposit by March 31st each year

Withdrawal Rules

  • Partial Withdrawal: Allowed after age 18 (for education)
  • Premature Closure: Only in exceptional cases (medical emergency, death)
  • Maturity: After 21 years from account opening
  • Extension: Account stops earning interest after maturity if not withdrawn

Penalty for Non-Payment

  • ₹50 penalty per year for not maintaining minimum ₹250
  • Account becomes inactive but can be revived with arrears + penalty

How to Open SSY Account in 2026

Required Documents

  1. Girl child's birth certificate
  2. Parent/guardian's KYC (Aadhaar, PAN)
  3. Address proof
  4. Passport-size photographs

Where to Open

  • Post Offices: Any India Post office
  • Banks: SBI, PNB, HDFC, ICICI, and other authorized banks
  • Online: Some banks offer digital account opening

Processing Time: Account opens same day with complete documentation

Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Mistake 1: Starting Late
Every year delay reduces maturity by approximately ₹1.5-2 lakh (for ₹50k yearly investment).
❌ Mistake 2: Irregular Deposits
Missing yearly deposits significantly impacts final corpus due to lost compounding.
❌ Mistake 3: Not Using Calculator
Parents often underestimate how much they need. Always calculate before investing.
❌ Mistake 4: Premature Withdrawal
Withdrawing before 21 years destroys the power of compounding.

Expert Tips to Maximize SSY Returns

💡 Tip 1: Start at Birth
Opening account when child is 0-1 years old maximizes the 21-year benefit period.
💡 Tip 2: Invest Maximum Early
If financially possible, invest higher amounts in initial years for better compounding.
💡 Tip 3: Combine with Other Investments
Use SSY for safety, and add equity mutual funds for potentially higher growth.
💡 Tip 4: Set Auto-Debit
Avoid missing deposits by setting up automatic yearly transfers.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I open SSY account for my 11-year-old daughter?
A: No, the girl child must be below 10 years at account opening.

Q: What happens if I miss a year's deposit?
A: You pay ₹50 penalty and can revive the account by paying minimum ₹250 + penalty for each missed year.

Q: Can NRIs open SSY account?
A: No, only resident Indians can open SSY accounts.

Q: Is interest rate fixed for 21 years?
A: No, the government reviews SSY interest rates quarterly. Current rate is 8.2% (2026).

Q: Can I transfer SSY account to another city?
A: Yes, accounts are fully transferable across India Post offices and authorized banks.

Final Verdict: Should You Invest in SSY?

Yes, if:

  • ✅ You have a daughter below 10 years
  • ✅ You want guaranteed returns with zero risk
  • ✅ You need tax savings under Section 80C
  • ✅ You're planning for education/marriage expenses

No, if:

  • ❌ You need liquidity before 21 years
  • ❌ You're looking for market-linked higher returns
  • ❌ Your daughter is already 10+ years old
Bottom Line: With 8.2% guaranteed returns, complete tax exemption, and government backing, SSY remains unbeatable for secure, long-term girl child planning. Use the calculator to plan your investments wisely and start early for maximum benefits.

Ready to Start? Visit your nearest post office or bank today, use the SSY calculator to decide your investment amount, and secure your daughter's future with confidence.
