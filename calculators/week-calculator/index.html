Understanding ISO Week Numbers

The ISO week number (ISO-8601 standard) is an international system for numbering weeks. It's widely used in business, project management, and international coordination.

ISO Week Rules:

  • Week 1 is the week containing the first Thursday of January
  • Weeks start on Monday and end on Sunday
  • A year has either 52 or 53 weeks
  • December 31 might belong to Week 1 of the next year

Why Use ISO Week Numbers?

  • International Business: Consistent week numbering across countries
  • Project Planning: Clear timeline communication
  • Manufacturing: Production scheduling
  • Reporting: Weekly reports and analytics

How Week Calculations Work

Weeks Between Dates Formula

Total Days = |End Date - Start Date|
Full Weeks = Total Days ÷ 7 (rounded down)
Remaining Days = Total Days mod 7

Add Weeks to Date Formula

New Date = Start Date + (Number of Weeks × 7 days)
